Civil Aviation Hackathon 2020

About Hackathaon:

There are over 40000 airports in the world which cater to billions of airline passengers and billions of tons of air freight/cargo. The Civil Aviation Hackathon is a challenge for students to come up with solutions to real world problems pertaining to civil Aviation (commercial, private and cargo). They can range from covering passenger journey facilitation to/from airport, in-airport experience at every touchpoint, air cargo handling, staff training and maintenance etc.

    • How does it work?

    If you are someone who wants to utilise your skills to solve these problems, then apply for this hackathon!


    Students of any stream are eligible to participate in the program. A minimum team size of 1 to a maximum size of 4 students are allowed to be part of a team. You can take part individually or in a team of up to 4 students.


    ROUND I

    In the first round, students will submit their ideas and after screening, 50 teams/individual participants will be selected for the second round.



    ROUND II

    The top 50 teams/individual participants from the first round will have a session they will pitch their idea to an elite panel which includes experts from civil aviation industry and out of the 50, 10 teams/individual participants will then head to the third round.


    ROUND III

    The top 10 performers will get a paid internship from Triangulum Labs, and will work on their idea before presenting it to the final jury. Winners will receive a cash prize in addition to opportunities.

    About Triangulum Labs

    Triangulam Labs helps entrepreneurs build their ideas into products and their dreams into startups, ready to raise funds and generate rapid customer growth, in addition to working with developers and entrepreneurs to cultivate ideas.

    Rewards

  • Top 10 (from Round 3) will be provided a well-paid internship at TriangulamLabs to develop Minimal Viable Product (MVP) of the idea
  • Among Top 10 the best idea will get a cash prize of 5 lakhs

  • Top 50 shortlisted ideas will get an Appreciation Certificate from Triangulum labs
